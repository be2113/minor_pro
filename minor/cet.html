          There are plenty of factors that contribute to making the sample papers for KCET a sure-shot solution for qualifying with good marks. We give you some of its benefits below:

          • Previous year question papers of KCET act as an aid in understanding the expected paper pattern and nature of questions.
          • These can, in turn, help you understand your weak and strong areas.
          • KCET Question Papers work best to work on your time, speed and accuracy.
          • The marking scheme that will be followed in the exam can also be understood beforehand. 

          The number of participants is fairly high in Karnataka CET. Candidates are selected for admission to various courses on the basis of merit only. The key to securing a good score and rank in KCET Exam is the awareness about the pattern of questions and topics that are frequently asked in the exam. Hence, it is recommended that you practice as many KCET Sample Papers as possible. 

          KCET 2020 will be conducted on April 22 to 24, 2020. KCET question papers and model tests should be incorporated by all the aspirants in their preparation plan. They help candidates identify the pattern of questions and important topics that carry maximum weightage.

            KCET 2020 Exam Pattern

            A brief about KCET Exam Pattern is given below to help candidates plan their preparation well:

            • Mode of Exam: The exam is held in offline mode (pen & paper based).
            • Types of Question: Question paper are of Multiple Choice in nature.
            • Number of Questions: Total 60 question is asked from each section.
            • Time Duration: Each section is given 80 minutes.
            • Marking Scheme: One mark is awarded for each correct answer.
            • Negative Marking: There is no provision for negative marking. No marks are deducted/awarded for marking multiple options.
            • KCET 2020 will be held on TWO days in FOUR different sessions for four subjects.
            • On the first day, the test will be conducted for Biology and Mathematics subjects in two sessions.
            • On day two, the test will be conducted for Physics and Chemistry subjects in two sessions as per the date and time notified.
